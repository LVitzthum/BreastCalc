Breast Cancer Post SLNB Risk Calculator

This tool is designed to aggregate existing prediction models for calculating a patient’s risk of having 4 or more axillary lymph nodes and additional lymph nodes after sentinel lymph node biopsy.

Katz Nomogram

This model incorporates tumor size, number of involved SLNs, presence of macromets, LVI, ECE, histology and number of negative SNLs to determine a patient’s risk of having 4 or more positive lymph nodes.

Katz et al. JCO 2008 PMID 18445838

Rivers Model

This model predicts the risk of having 4 or more positive axillary lymph nodes in patients that had 1 to 3 positive SNL based on tumor size, number of positive SLNs, percent of positive SLNs and the absence or presence of LVI and ECE.

Rivers et al. Ann Surg Oncol. 2006 PMID 16378156

External Validaiton of Models

These models were evaluated by Zendejas and colleagues and were found to demonstrate good calibration to an independent patient population.

Zendejas et al. Ann Surg Oncol. 2010 PMID 20429038
